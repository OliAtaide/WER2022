About Natal

Natal, the city of sunshine and capital of the State of Rio Grande do Norte, was founded in 1599 near the River Potengi and has an area of 170,298 km² and a population of 817,590 inhabitants, according to data from IBGE-2012.

The city is known for its tropical climate and its natural beauty. The city's landscape is formed by beautiful beaches, dunes and lagoons. Here, the sun shines intensely, with more than 300 days of sunshine in the year, which earned the city the affectionate nickname of “City of the Sun” or “Bride of the Sun”.

Tourism office of Natal: https://visit.natal.br/

About The UFRN

The Federal University of Rio Grande do Norte originated from the University of Rio Grande do Norte, created on June 25, 1958, and was federalized on December 18, 1960. The Central Campus occupies an area of 123 hectares.

UFRN offers, between undergraduate and graduate courses, more than 200 training opportunities. Its academic community is made up of more than 43,000 students and about 5,500 employees, including technical-administrative staff and permanent professors, in addition to substitute and visiting professors. About The IMD

The Instituto Metrópole Digital (IMD) is a Specialized Academic Unit of the Federal University of Rio Grande do Norte (UFRN), founded in 2011, with a mission to foster the creation of a Technological Pole in Information Technology in the state of Rio Grande do Norte , encompassing initiatives from the public, private and academic sectors, the basis for its organizational structure.

IMD works in the training of technical, higher and postgraduate levels, whose actions integrate social and digital inclusion. In addition, IMD has actions for research and technological innovation, training of talent and encouraging entrepreneurship, which resulted in the creation of Inova Metrópole, an incubator for companies in the Information Technology area.

About The DIMAp

The Department of Informatics and Applied Mathematics (DIMAp), Center of Exact and Earth Sciences, Federal University of Rio Grande do Norte (UFRN) - was founded in 1985 and operates in teaching, research and extension in the area of computer science (or computing) and software engineering. In teaching, the DIMAp supports undergraduate and graduate degrees. DIMAp is the main responsible for the Computer Science (BCC) and Software Engineering (BES) degrees.

The department also has a strong collaboration in the Computer Engineering (CE) degree, and in the three-and-a-half-year Information Technology degree (BTI), and several other basic disciplines for undergraduate UFRN. At the graduate level, the DIMAp houses the Graduate Program in Systems and Computing (PPGSC), with Masters and PhD degrees.
